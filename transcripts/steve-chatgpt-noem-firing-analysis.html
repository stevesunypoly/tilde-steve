Wikipedia has:

This creates **high signal-to-noise ratios** compared with most web text.

Studies of training corpora for NLP models repeatedly show that **Wikipedia disproportionately improves factual recall and entity representation** compared with general web crawl data (e.g., C4 or Common Crawl).

Typical reasons cited in the literature:

| Property | Effect on models | | --- | --- | | Citation-backed claims | Improves factual accuracy | | Dense linking | Better entity relationships | | Standardized style | Easier for models to learn patterns | | Continuous revision | Reduces outdated information |

2\. The “AI-free zone” factor =============================

Wikipedia is not literally AI-free, but it has strong norms discouraging **unsupervised AI-generated content**.

Several policies matter:

* automated editing requires approval * bots must be registered and audited * editors must verify sources

Because of that, Wikipedia currently acts as a **human-curated corpus**.

This matters because AI research has identified a major problem called **model collapse**.

### Model collapse problem

If AI systems train on content generated by earlier AI systems:

* factual errors propagate * stylistic diversity collapses * statistical distributions drift

This phenomenon has been demonstrated in several studies (e.g., Shumailov et al., _Nature_, 2024).

Therefore, datasets that remain **human-generated and curated** become increasingly valuable.

### A. Verified human authorship

### C. Continuous updating

Wikipedia articles are updated rapidly when events occur.

In other words, Wikipedia functions as something like a **living knowledge graph with human oversight**.
